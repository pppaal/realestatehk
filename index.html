Hong Kong Legal System

The Basic Law is Hong Kong's mini-constitution. It guarantees "one country, two systems" and preserves Hong Kong's autonomy and legal system until 2047.

Sources of Law

  • Common Law: Judge-made law based on precedents.
  • Equity Law: Focuses on fairness and justice.
  • Statutory Law: Laws passed by the legislature.

Judiciary

The judiciary is independent and interprets and enforces the laws of Hong Kong. It ensures the rule of law and upholds the Basic Law.

Hierarchy of Courts

  • Court of Final Appeal (CFA): The highest court in Hong Kong.
  • High Court:
    • Court of Appeal
    • Court of First Instance
  • District Court: Handles mid-level civil and criminal cases.
  • Magistrates' Courts: Handle minor criminal cases.
  • Tribunals: Specialized courts for labor, land, etc.
